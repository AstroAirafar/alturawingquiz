The Man Behind the Legacy

Photo by Wmgwarwick, via Wikimedia Commons.
Licensed under CC BY-SA 3.0.

Ratan Naval Tata was an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and the chairman of Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012. Under his visionary leadership, the Tata Group transformed from a largely India-centric company into a global powerhouse with operations in over 100 countries.

Born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai into the illustrious Tata family, Ratan Tata graduated with a degree in architecture from Cornell University in 1962. He later attended the Advanced Management Program at Harvard Business School.

He joined Tata Industries in 1962 and worked on the shop floor of Tata Steel in Jamshedpur. Over three decades, he rose through the ranks, taking the helm of Tata Sons in 1991 from J.R.D. Tata. During his 21-year tenure as chairman, he led the group's revenue to grow over 40 times, from $5.7 billion to $100 billion.

He was honored with Padma Bhushan (2000) and Padma Vibhushan (2008) — India's third and second-highest civilian honors, respectively. Ratan Tata passed away on October 9, 2024, at the age of 86 in Mumbai, leaving behind a legacy of ethical business and transformative philanthropy.

Key Milestones

1937
Born on December 28 in Mumbai, Maharashtra
1962
Graduated from Cornell University with a B.Arch degree; joined Tata Group
1991
Appointed Chairman of Tata Sons, succeeding J.R.D. Tata
1998
Launched the Tata Indica — India's first truly indigenous car
2000
Tata Tea acquires Tetley, marking Tata Group's first major international acquisition
2007
Tata Steel acquires Corus Group for $12.1 billion — the largest Indian acquisition of a foreign company
2008
Tata Motors acquires Jaguar Land Rover from Ford for $2.3 billion; Tata Nano unveiled; Received Padma Vibhushan
2012
Stepped down as Chairman of Tata Sons; succeeded by Cyrus Mistry
2024
Passed away on October 9 at the age of 86 in Mumbai
